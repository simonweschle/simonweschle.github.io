Beyond the Coalition Heuristic: How Voters Infer Parties' Policy Positions based on Elite Interactions.

with James Adams and Christopher Wlezien

Remarkable research by Fortunato and Stevenson documents that voters infer that governing coalition partners share similar ideologies, independently of these parties' actual policy statements. We extend this perspective to ask whether voters respond more generally to the cooperative and conflictual behavior between different political parties (as well as party interactions with non-political actors). Based on analyses of over 200,000 media reports on inter-party bargaining and elites’ public statements from twelve European democracies between 2001 and 2014, we construct measures of inter-party conflict and cooperation, and we show that - when controlling for governing coalition arrangements and for the policy content of parties' election manifestos - voters infer greater policy agreement between pairs of parties that exhibit more cooperative interactions. Our finding that citizens employ this cooperation heuristic has implications for parties’ policy images and for mass-elite linkages.
